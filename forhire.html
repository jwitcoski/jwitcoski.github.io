Mapbox Application

Aprilish, 2016| Category: Jobs|

Introduction

I am a professional geographer employed for almost a decade. After working most of my career in using propriety software I wish to break from these constraints and continue my career in a more challenging and productive open source field. Every time Mapbox releases a new programming library or software I am amazed. I am continuously looking at your technology and not your competitors to create maps in my free time. I am tired of jumping from company to company to get new challenges and skills. Mapbox is a growing company filled with engaging talent that I would love to be apart of.

Please consider me for the following positions: Government sales representative, Sales engineer, and Data Engineer

About me

Undergraduate

In 2004 I graduated from Penn State with degrees in Geography and Anthropology.


Below is a leafletjs map that shows all the data collected from an archaeological project in Greece. It runs slow due since all the data needs to load.

Masters

In 2007 I graduated with a Masters in Geography focused on the Theory of Central Place from the University of Tennessee.
Graduate school taught me how to write well and further developed my GIS skill-sets.


Below is a leafletjs map that shows firestations in dc and their boundaries. These were placed on top of Mapbox's isodistance example to allow you to detect if firestations can reach every part of their district.

Tyco Electronics Subcommunication Geospatial Analyst

One of my first jobs was producing/consolidating data and maps for an underwater telecommunications firm. I made desktop and server products using products from ESRI, AutoCAD, ERDAS, and Intergraph. The company had me using a range of software based upon the current need and budget.


Below is a leafletjs map that shows all the underwater telecommunication cables in the world.

Infrastructure Protection Geospatial Analyst

After working with Engineers, I started working on a federal government contract with Booz Allen producing static maps for homeland security using consolidated datasets under a project called HIFLD. I got to work remotely on a team throughout the country using local, state, and national datasets to highlight key infrastructure for emergency and big ticket events. I helped get some of the nationally collected datasets now known as HIFLD Open.


Here is a simple map using one of the Mapbox tutorials that show Government (HIFLD Open) vs Crowd sourced (OSM) data.

Army National Guard Geospatial Software Engineer

The position I had for five years was to develop and produce paper maps. Seeing the growing demand for GIS profressionals with web based skill sets I made a transition in 2014 beginning work as a GIS windows system administrator developing GIS servers to be deployed in the DOD environment.

  • I learned about patching servers and cyber security (ports, firewalls, and STIGs) using system admin tools (Bash, Manage Engine, VMware)

  • Developed my Technical Writing skills by writing items such as build guides and testing documents for how to successfully deploy an ESRI solutions dataserver, webserver, and license server.

  • I became the GIS software support for any software related question for the Army National Guard 54 states and territories GIS staff.

  • I learned about how to manage and document my time in an Agile Software Development Methodology.

    Other Activities

    In addition to my workload I participated in OpenStreetMap, adding data to the servers via HOT, my hometown, and vacation destinations
    here is a quick map of my contributions.

    I participated in the first HOT Summit by talking about Using OpenStreetMap to Map Hazardous areas.

    Conclusion

    I have been viewing Mapbox's social media posts and press releases as well as talking with your team members during happy hours for the last two years. This has inspired me to develop my skillsets to someday work with such an innovative team. I have focused on a wide range of skills that you have listed under your current job positions and given support would master any of them if given the opportunity. It pains me to be working on software from your rivals like ESRI that while innovative only take steps to improve their software once they feel threatened by other's innovation.

    I am applying for the following positions: Government sales representative, Sales engineer, and Data Engineer

    I look forward to the challenges any of those positions I mentioned and look forward to hearing more from your team
